# Chapter 13: Gravitation

Gravitation governs the motion of planets, satellites, and objects under the influence of massive bodies like Earth. This chapter explores Newton’s law of gravitation, gravitational potential energy, the motion of planets and satellites, and Kepler’s laws of planetary motion. These concepts are critical for JEE Main, JEE Advanced, and NEET, appearing in problems involving orbital velocities, escape speeds, and planetary orbits. With clear explanations, exam-focused examples, and robust problem-solving strategies, this chapter equips students to master gravitation in competitive exams, ensuring precision and conceptual clarity.

## 13.1 Newton’s Law of Gravitation

Newton’s law of gravitation states that every mass attracts every other mass with a force proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between their centers: \( F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \), where \( G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{N·m}^2/\text{kg}^2 \) is the gravitational constant, \( m_1 \), \( m_2 \) are masses, and \( r \) is the separation. The force is always attractive, acting along the line joining the masses. JEE/NEET problems often involve calculating gravitational forces or accelerations. *Fundamentals of Physics* emphasizes vector forms for multiple masses.

**Solved Example**: A JEE Main problem involves two 10 kg masses 2 m apart. Calculate the gravitational force between them.
- **Solution**: Using Newton’s law: \( F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \). Given \( m_1 = m_2 = 10 \, \text{kg} \), \( r = 2 \, \text{m} \), \( G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{N·m}^2/\text{kg}^2 \): \( F = \frac{6.674 \times 10^{-11} \times 10 \times 10}{2^2} = \frac{6.674 \times 10^{-9}}{4} = 1.6685 \times 10^{-9} \, \text{N} \approx 1.7 \times 10^{-9} \, \text{N} \) (2 significant figures, April 14, 2025). This is a standard JEE force calculation.
  - **JEE Tip**: Use SI units; small \( F \) reflects weak gravitational force. Common error: Incorrect powers of 10 for \( G \).

**Solved Example**: A NEET problem involves a 5 kg mass on Earth’s surface (radius \( 6.371 \times 10^6 \, \text{m} \), mass \( 5.972 \times 10^{24} \, \text{kg} \)). Calculate the gravitational acceleration.
- **Solution**: Gravitational acceleration: \( g = \frac{G M}{R^2} \). Given \( M = 5.972 \times 10^{24} \, \text{kg} \), \( R = 6.371 \times 10^6 \, \text{m} \), \( G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{N·m}^2/\text{kg}^2 \): \( g = \frac{6.674 \times 10^{-11} \times 5.972 \times 10^{24}}{(6.371 \times 10^6)^2} \approx \frac{3.985 \times 10^{14}}{4.059 \times 10^{13}} \approx 9.817 \, \text{m/s}^2 \approx 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2 \) (2 significant figures). This is a typical NEET gravity problem.
  - **NEET Tip**: Approximate \( g \approx 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2 \); use Earth’s parameters. Common error: Using incorrect radius or mass.

## 13.2 Gravitational Potential Energy

Gravitational potential energy between two masses is \( U = -\frac{G m_1 m_2}{r} \), where \( r \) is the distance between their centers. The negative sign indicates a bound system, with \( U = 0 \) at infinite separation. JEE/NEET problems involve calculating \( U \), work done, or potential energy changes for orbits. *University Physics* highlights applications in satellite launches, tested in JEE Advanced.

**Solved Example**: A JEE Advanced problem involves a 1000 kg satellite at 500 km above Earth’s surface (Earth’s mass \( 5.972 \times 10^{24} \, \text{kg} \), radius \( 6.371 \times 10^6 \, \text{m} \)). Calculate the gravitational potential energy.
- **Solution**: Distance from Earth’s center: \( r = 6.371 \times 10^6 + 5 \times 10^5 = 6.871 \times 10^6 \, \text{m} \). Potential energy: \( U = -\frac{G M m}{r} \). Given \( M = 5.972 \times 10^{24} \, \text{kg} \), \( m = 1000 \, \text{kg} \), \( G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{N·m}^2/\text{kg}^2 \): \( U = -\frac{6.674 \times 10^{-11} \times 5.972 \times 10^{24} \times 1000}{6.871 \times 10^6} \approx -\frac{3.985 \times 10^{17}}{6.871 \times 10^6} \approx -5.801 \times 10^{10} \, \text{J} \approx -5.8 \times 10^{10} \, \text{J} \) (2 significant figures). This is a classic JEE orbital problem.
  - **JEE Tip**: Include Earth’s radius in \( r \); negative \( U \) indicates bound system. Common error: Using surface radius.

**Solved Example**: A NEET problem involves two 2 kg masses moving from 1 m to 2 m apart. Calculate the work done by the gravitational force.
- **Solution**: Work done by gravity equals the negative change in potential energy: \( W = -\Delta U = U_f - U_i \). Initial: \( U_i = -\frac{G m_1 m_2}{r_i} = -\frac{6.674 \times 10^{-11} \times 2 \times 2}{1} = -2.6696 \times 10^{-10} \, \text{J} \). Final: \( U_f = -\frac{6.674 \times 10^{-11} \times 2 \times 2}{2} = -1.3348 \times 10^{-10} \, \text{J} \). Work: \( W = -1.3348 \times 10^{-10} - (-2.6696 \times 10^{-10}) = 1.3348 \times 10^{-10} \, \text{J} \approx 1.3 \times 10^{-10} \, \text{J} \) (2 significant figures). This is a typical NEET work problem.
  - **NEET Tip**: Work is positive when separation increases. Common error: Forgetting negative sign in \( U \).

## 13.3 Motion of Planets and Satellites

Planets and satellites move under gravitational forces, often in circular or elliptical orbits. For a circular orbit, centripetal force is provided by gravity: \( \frac{m v^2}{r} = \frac{G M m}{r^2} \), yielding orbital velocity \( v = \sqrt{\frac{G M}{r}} \). Escape speed is \( v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2 G M}{r}} \). JEE/NEET problems involve orbital speeds, periods, or escape velocities. *University Physics* connects to space missions, tested in JEE Advanced.

**Solved Example**: A JEE Main problem involves a satellite orbiting Earth at 400 km above the surface (Earth’s mass \( 5.972 \times 10^{24} \, \text{kg} \), radius \( 6.371 \times 10^6 \, \text{m} \)). Calculate the orbital velocity.
- **Solution**: Orbital radius: \( r = 6.371 \times 10^6 + 4 \times 10^5 = 6.771 \times 10^6 \, \text{m} \). Orbital velocity: \( v = \sqrt{\frac{G M}{r}} = \sqrt{\frac{6.674 \times 10^{-11} \times 5.972 \times 10^{24}}{6.771 \times 10^6}} \approx \sqrt{\frac{3.985 \times 10^{14}}{6.771 \times 10^6}} \approx \sqrt{5.887 \times 10^7} \approx 7673.5 \, \text{m/s} \approx 7.7 \times 10^3 \, \text{m/s} \) (2 significant figures). This is a standard JEE satellite problem.
  - **JEE Tip**: Use total radius from Earth’s center; simplify units. Common error: Using surface radius.

**Solved Example**: A NEET problem involves an object on Earth’s surface (mass \( 5.972 \times 10^{24} \, \text{kg} \), radius \( 6.371 \times 10^6 \, \text{m} \)). Calculate the escape speed.
- **Solution**: Escape speed: \( v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2 G M}{R}} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \times 5.972 \times 10^{24}}{6.371 \times 10^6}} \approx \sqrt{\frac{7.97 \times 10^{14}}{6.371 \times 10^6}} \approx \sqrt{1.251 \times 10^8} \approx 11184 \, \text{m/s} \approx 11.2 \, \text{km/s} \) (3 significant figures). This is a common NEET escape speed problem.
  - **NEET Tip**: Factor of 2 distinguishes escape from orbital speed. Common error: Using orbital velocity formula.

## 13.4 Kepler’s Laws of Planetary Motion

Kepler’s laws describe planetary orbits:
1. **Law of Ellipses**: Planets move in elliptical orbits with the Sun at one focus.
2. **Law of Equal Areas**: A line from the planet to the Sun sweeps equal areas in equal times, implying faster motion near the Sun.
3. **Law of Periods**: The square of the orbital period \( T \) is proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis \( a \): \( T^2 = \frac{4 \pi^2}{G M} a^3 \).
JEE/NEET problems involve applying these laws to orbits or periods. *Fundamentals of Physics* connects to satellite orbits, tested in JEE Advanced.

**Solved Example**: A JEE Advanced problem involves a satellite in a circular orbit (radius \( 7 \times 10^6 \, \text{m} \)) around Earth (mass \( 5.972 \times 10^{24} \, \text{kg} \)). Calculate the orbital period.
- **Solution**: For a circular orbit, semi-major axis \( a = r = 7 \times 10^6 \, \text{m} \). Kepler’s third law: \( T^2 = \frac{4 \pi^2}{G M} a^3 \). Given \( G = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{N·m}^2/\text{kg}^2 \), \( M = 5.972 \times 10^{24} \, \text{kg} \): \( G M = 6.674 \times 10^{-11} \times 5.972 \times 10^{24} \approx 3.985 \times 10^{14} \, \text{m}^3/\text{s}^2 \). Then, \( T^2 = \frac{4 \times (3.1416)^2 \times (7 \times 10^6)^3}{3.985 \times 10^{14}} \approx \frac{39.478 \times 3.43 \times 10^{20}}{3.985 \times 10^{14}} \approx 3.398 \times 10^7 \). So, \( T = \sqrt{3.398 \times 10^7} \approx 5829.8 \, \text{s} \approx 5830 \, \text{s} \approx 97 \, \text{min} \) (2 significant figures). This is a classic JEE period problem.
  - **JEE Tip**: For circular orbits, \( a = r \); simplify \( G M \). Common error: Incorrect cube of radius.

**Solved Example**: A NEET problem involves a planet with twice Earth’s period around the Sun (Earth’s period 1 year, semi-major axis \( 1.496 \times 10^{11} \, \text{m} \)). Calculate the planet’s semi-major axis.
- **Solution**: Kepler’s third law: \( \frac{T_1^2}{a_1^3} = \frac{T_2^2}{a_2^3} \). Earth: \( T_1 = 1 \, \text{yr} \), \( a_1 = 1.496 \times 10^{11} \, \text{m} \). Planet: \( T_2 = 2 \, \text{yr} \). Thus, \( \frac{1^2}{(1.496 \times 10^{11})^3} = \frac{2^2}{a_2^3} \), \( \frac{1}{3.351 \times 10^{33}} = \frac{4}{a_2^3} \). Solve: \( a_2^3 = 4 \times 3.351 \times 10^{33} \approx 1.34 \times 10^{34} \), \( a_2 = (1.34 \times 10^{34})^{1/3} \approx 2.375 \times 10^{11} \, \text{m} \approx 2.4 \times 10^{11} \, \text{m} \) (2 significant figures). This is a typical NEET Kepler problem.
  - **NEET Tip**: Use ratio form to avoid constants. Common error: Incorrect exponent in cube.

## Review & Summary
- **Newton’s Law**: \( F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \); universal attractive force.
- **Potential Energy**: \( U = -\frac{G m_1 m_2}{r} \); negative for bound systems.
- **Orbital Motion**: Circular orbit velocity \( v = \sqrt{\frac{G M}{r}} \); escape speed \( v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2 G M}{r}} \).
- **Kepler’s Laws**: Elliptical orbits, equal areas, \( T^2 \propto a^3 \).

## Quick Revision Section
- **Key Equations**:
  - Gravitational force: \( F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \)
  - Potential energy: \( U = -\frac{G m_1 m_2}{r} \)
  - Orbital velocity: \( v = \sqrt{\frac{G M}{r}} \)
  - Escape speed: \( v_e = \sqrt{\frac{2 G M}{r}} \)
  - Kepler’s third law: \( T^2 = \frac{4 \pi^2}{G M} a^3 \)
- **SI Units**: Force (N), energy (J), distance (m), velocity (m/s), period (s).
- **JEE/NEET Tips**: Use Earth’s parameters (\( M \approx 5.972 \times 10^{24} \, \text{kg} \), \( R \approx 6.371 \times 10^6 \, \text{m} \)), verify significant figures (April 14, 2025), include radius in orbital calculations, simplify Kepler’s law with ratios.
